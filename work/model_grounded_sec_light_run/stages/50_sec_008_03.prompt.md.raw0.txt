Looking at this HARA analysis scenario for low beam headlight malfunction:

**Scenario Summary:**
- Hazard: Low beam (近光灯) unexpectedly turns off (MF105)
- Context: Daytime urban expressway tunnel, 60-90 km/h, close to vehicle ahead
- Harm: Rear-end collision due to insufficient vision after sudden light loss

## SEC Analysis

**Severity S3:**
- Speed 60-90 km/h exceeds the ≥40 km/h threshold for life-threatening rear-end collisions
- Tunnel collision leaves no escape route, increasing force
- Multiple occupants at risk (both vehicles)

**Exposure E2:**
- Urban expressway with tunnel is a common driving scenario, but:
  - Not every urban commute involves tunnels
  - Daytime tunnel entry is frequent but not "almost every drive"
  - 0.4-4 hours/year range seems too conservative; tunnels in urban areas are weekly or monthly occurrences for many commuters
  - This is likely E3 (medium probability, 4-40 hours/year) - monthly occurrence for typical urban driver

**Controllability C3:**
- Sudden vision loss in already low-light tunnel environment
- Reaction time reduced by proximity to vehicle ahead ("较近")
- Driver has 2-4 seconds typically to detect and react to decelerating front vehicle
- Tunnel environment means no escape route, limited maneuvering space
- Vision loss is unexpected and counterintuitive

## ASIL Rating
S3 (3) + E3 (2) + C3 (3) = 8 → **ASIL B**
